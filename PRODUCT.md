# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Developers on the Claude Max plan who hit the 5-hour usage window mid-workday. They find the project via GitHub or word of mouth, skim the landing page in under a minute, and decide whether to set it up. Context: technical, skeptical of fluff, allergic to marketing-speak — but they get the joke.

## Product Purpose

Good Morning, Vietnam is a tiny open-source automation (Python + GitHub Actions) that starts your Claude Max 5-hour usage window before you wake up, by sending Claude an automated "good morning" the moment the previous window expires. Runs in the cloud — your computer stays off. Success: a visitor understands the trick in 10 seconds, stars the repo, and completes setup in 5 minutes.

## Brand Personality

Loud, irreverent, precise. The voice of a 1960s radio DJ shouting the morning awake — Robin Williams at the AFVN mic — applied to a deadpan-practical dev tool. Big poster energy on the surface, engineering rigor underneath. Emotional goals: a grin, then "oh, that's actually clever."

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient hero, identical feature card grids, hero-metric stats.
- Cream/sand "editorial minimal" AI-default look.
- Dark "terminal dev tool" aesthetic (deliberately rejected by the owner in favor of the poster direction).
- Corporate Anthropic-clone styling — this is a fan-made hack, not a product pitch.

## Design Principles

1. **The poster IS the pitch** — one giant typographic shout should communicate the product before a single paragraph is read.
2. **Joke first, proof immediately after** — every playful beat is followed by a concrete technical fact (cron, token, skip logic).
3. **One page, one action** — everything funnels to the GitHub repo; no nav maze, no secondary CTAs competing.
4. **Period flavor, not costume party** — 1960s poster cues (type, color, texture) applied with restraint; the page must still read as a sharp modern site.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: 4.5:1 body contrast even on saturated poster backgrounds, visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives for all animation, semantic headings, keyboard-reachable everything.
